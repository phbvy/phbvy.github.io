Product Design

Problem Description

Hitta.se is Sweden's leading people and business directory, used by millions of Swedes. Hitta.se also offers Swedish business owners a suite of tools that help them manage and grow their online presence.

Business owners were struggling to manage two critical aspects of their online presence separately: responding to customer reviews spread across multiple platforms, and keeping up a consistent social media presence. I was brought in to design a unified B2B product that brings both workflows into a single, cohesive platform.

About Hitta.se

Sweden's leading online directory for finding people, businesses, and addresses — and a growing B2B platform helping businesses manage their digital presence.

Context

Who the Users Are

Small and medium business owners — restaurants, salons, clinics, retailers — who want to build their online reputation but lack the time or tools to manage reviews and social media separately.

The Opportunity

Hitta.se is already where customers look up businesses. The opportunity was to build a management layer on top of that, so businesses could control their presence right where the attention already is.

Design

Presence Overview

A summary dashboard showing average review rating, recent review activity, and upcoming scheduled posts — giving business owners a quick health check of their online presence at a glance.

Review Inbox

A unified inbox aggregating reviews from all connected platforms, with a clear unread/responded status so nothing slips through. Responding is a single inline action — no platform switching required.

Social Composer

A lightweight post composer that lets business owners write once and publish to multiple connected channels simultaneously, with an optional scheduling calendar for planned posts.

Afterthoughts

Designing a B2B product for time-constrained business owners reinforced the importance of task focus over feature completeness:

1

B2B users are goal-oriented and time-scarce. Every extra step in a workflow has a real cost — B2B design must prioritise getting the user to completion as fast as possible.

2

Combining two distinct workflows into one product only works if the unification creates genuine value. Reviews and social posting share the same underlying need — a business's public voice — which is what made the combination coherent rather than arbitrary.
